# Monthly Sale Banner Concepts — April/May 2026

**Drafted:** 2026-04-10
**Purpose:** Replace the current GameCube banner on the homepage. You design the chosen concept in Affinity.

---

## Brand anchors (all concepts should honor these)

- **Primary color:** 8-Bit Legacy orange `#ff9526`
- **Accent:** sky-blue `#0EA5E9`
- **Font family:** Nunito (matches dashboard brand) + optionally a pixel-art font for accent words
- **Feel:** Nintendo Switch-era clean, not cluttered retro-kitsch
- **Must include:** logo + CTA + URL (8bitlegacy.com)

---

## Concept 1 — "Starter Pack" (RECOMMENDED)

**Theme:** Direct alignment with the Layer 1 sale (under-$20, 15% off).

**Layout:**
- Left side: stacked grid of 6-9 classic cartridges and jewel cases at varied angles (NES, SNES, Game Boy, PS1 — a mix). Slight drop shadow, photographed against a neutral backdrop or rendered in pixel-art style.
- Right side: big bold headline + CTA.

**Copy (tested options, pick one):**
- `"START YOUR COLLECTION — 15% OFF EVERYTHING UNDER $20"`
- `"RETRO STARTER PACK — 15% OFF UNDER $20"`
- `"BUILD YOUR BACKLOG — 15% OFF EVERYTHING UNDER $20"`

**CTA button:** "Shop Starter Collection" → links to a filtered collection page (`/collections/all?filter.price.lte=20`)

**Color:** White background, orange accent bar, sky-blue button

**Why this concept:** It's tied directly to the sale layer structure in the sale-wave-plan doc. Cold visitors see a clear, low-commitment entry point. Works well for first-time buyer psychology.

---

## Concept 2 — "Deals of the Week" rotating hero

**Theme:** A weekly-rotating banner that highlights 3-4 specific iconic titles on sale.

**Layout:**
- Full-width hero
- 3-4 large product cards in a row, each showing a game's box art + sale price strike-through
- Headline above: "DEALS OF THE WEEK — 20% OFF"
- Small text below: "New deals every Monday"

**Copy options:**
- `"DEALS OF THE WEEK — 20% OFF"`
- `"THIS WEEK'S LINEUP — 20% OFF"`
- `"WEEKLY RESPAWN — 20% OFF"`

**CTA:** "Shop This Week's Deals" → `/collections/deals-of-the-week` (create this collection)

**Why this concept:** Creates urgency ("new deals every Monday"), gives repeat visitors a reason to come back, matches Layer 2 of the sale plan.

**Downside:** Requires updating the banner weekly. Higher maintenance than Concept 1. Consider this a secondary banner below the hero, not the main hero itself.

---

## Concept 3 — "Console Spotlight" (podcast-tied)

**Theme:** Matches the biweekly podcast cycle. Each banner spotlights one console.

**Layout:**
- Left: hero image of the console itself (vector illustration or high-quality photo) + silhouettes of 3-4 iconic games for that console in the background
- Right: bold console name + sale details + CTA

**Copy:**
- `"N64 COLLECTION — 12% OFF ALL NINTENDO 64"`
- `"BRING THE N64 HOME — 12% OFF"`
- `"THE NINTENDO 64 SALE — 12% OFF TOP TITLES"`

**CTA:** "Shop N64 Games" → `/collections/n64-games`

**Rotation:** Change the banner console every 2 weeks to match podcast episodes (N64 → PS1 → SNES → Genesis → GBA → GameCube).

**Why this concept:** Content-to-commerce alignment with the podcast. Gives podcast listeners a direct shopping hook.

**Downside:** Requires rebuilding the banner every 2 weeks. Only use if you're committed to the biweekly design cadence.

---

## Concept 4 — "Free Shipping + Sale Combo"

**Theme:** Lead with the free shipping threshold (already in place) and pair it with the under-$20 sale.

**Layout:**
- Full-width text-heavy banner
- Big bold: "FREE SHIPPING OVER $50"
- Sub: "+ 15% OFF ANY GAME UNDER $20"
- Small: "Stack the deals. Start your collection."
- Background: subtle retro dithering pattern in brand orange

**CTA:** "Shop the Sale"

**Why this concept:** Combines two triggers (free shipping + discount) in one message. Simpler to execute than image-heavy concepts. Works on mobile better than the others.

**Downside:** Text-heavy, less visually striking than concepts 1 or 3.

---

## Concept 5 — "Podcast + Store" crossover

**Theme:** Launch banner that introduces the podcast + sale together.

**Layout:**
- Hero split: podcast art on left (3-person silhouettes around a retro TV), store product grid on right
- Headline: `"THE 8-BIT LEGACY PODCAST IS HERE"`
- Subhead: `"New episodes every other week — plus 15% off your first order"`
- Two CTAs: "Watch EP 1" + "Shop the Sale"

**Why this concept:** Double promotion — builds podcast audience and store traffic. Good for the initial launch of both.

**Downside:** Busier than the others. Podcast only has 1 episode so far; the banner may feel premature.

---

## Recommendation

**Lead with Concept 1 ("Starter Pack")** as the main homepage hero. It's the simplest to design, most on-strategy, and requires no weekly maintenance.

**Secondary placement:** Use a smaller version of Concept 2 ("Deals of the Week") as a second-tier banner below the hero if you want the weekly-refresh hook. Update it via Shopify theme editor every Monday.

**Skip for now:** Concepts 3, 4, 5. Revisit Concept 3 in Month 2 once the podcast has more episodes and the console spotlight cadence is established.

---

## Design specs (for Affinity)

- **Dimensions:** 1920×600 px (standard Shopify hero width, scales down on mobile)
- **Mobile dimensions (separate file):** 750×900 px (vertical crop with larger text)
- **Format:** PNG with transparent option or high-quality JPG
- **File size target:** <300KB for desktop, <150KB for mobile (compress via Squoosh or similar)
- **Text safe zones:** keep critical text in the center 60% of the frame — sides may be cropped on ultrawide displays
- **Font suggestion:** Nunito Bold for main copy, Press Start 2P (pixel font) for accent words only
- **Export:** sRGB color profile, 72 DPI

---

## Export checklist

When you're ready to upload:

- [ ] Desktop version: 1920×600
